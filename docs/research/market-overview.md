# EU Property Valuation Landscape

## Overview

Automated property valuation models (AVMs) and address-based price maps vary dramatically across the EU. Accuracy depends heavily on data transparency -- countries where governments publish final sale prices have excellent tools, while opaque markets rely on inflated asking prices or have no tools at all.

## Target Countries (Gap Markets)

### Malta -- No AVM Exists
- Tiny market, extreme property variance (400-year-old palazzo next to modern apartment)
- No open public transaction data
- Manual valuations only: fill a form, architect (Perit) calculates and emails back
- Portals: PropertyMarket.com.mt, Valur.mt (all human-driven)

### Cyprus -- Barely Exists
- Relies entirely on manual RICS professional valuations
- Portal "instant valuations" are rudimentary lead-gen calculators based on area averages
- Not intelligent data-driven algorithms

### Bulgaria -- Very Limited
- General price maps exist (Imot.bg) but based entirely on asking prices
- No public access to actual closed sale prices
- Opaque public registry

### Croatia -- Very Limited
- CroReal has price maps but asking-price-based only
- True AVMs predicting sale price from historical transactions unavailable to public
- Opaque registry system

## Opportunity

PriceMap targets these 4 countries specifically because they represent the largest gap in EU coverage. By combining listing data scraping, official statistical indices, and ML-based hedonic pricing models, we can provide the first automated, data-driven property valuations for these markets.
