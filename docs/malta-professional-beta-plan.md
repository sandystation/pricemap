# PriceMap Malta Professional Beta Plan

## Summary

Validate PriceMap in Malta with professional users first: Periti, valuers, property consultants, buyer agents, and small agencies. The first product promise is not a formal automated valuation. It is a comparable-evidence assistant that helps professionals sanity-check asking prices, find relevant listings faster, and explain valuation reasoning.

Primary 30-day goal: prove that five Malta professionals will repeatedly use PriceMap for real valuation-support work.

Success criteria:

- 10 professional discovery calls.
- 5 active beta users.
- 50 submitted properties.
- 20 rated outputs marked usable, directionally useful, or not useful.
- 2 professionals willing to pay or continue weekly usage after the beta.

## Positioning

Use:

- "Comparable evidence assistant for Malta property professionals."
- "First-pass market range and comp discovery."
- "Listing-data support for valuation research."
- "Find pricing anomalies and explain local market context faster."

Avoid:

- "Bank-grade AVM."
- "Formal valuation."
- "Perit replacement."
- "Verified transaction-data model" unless PPR or other closed-sale data is licensed and integrated.

Standard disclaimer:

> PriceMap provides valuation-support analysis based on available listing data, comparable properties, location, and property features. It is not a formal Perit valuation, bank valuation, legal advice, or a substitute for professional judgment.

## Outreach Plan

Week 1: Prepare beta material.

- Build a short private-beta page or deck with the positioning above.
- Include one sample Malta report showing estimate range, comparables, confidence, caveats, and data provenance.
- Prepare a spreadsheet/CRM with source, contact, role, outreach date, call date, properties submitted, output rating, objections, and payment signal.

Week 2: Contact 40-60 professionals.

- Periti and architecture practices advertising valuation services.
- Independent valuation/property consultants.
- Buyer-side agents and investment advisors.
- Small real estate agencies that publish market commentary or buyer guides.

Outreach message:

> I am building PriceMap, a Malta property valuation-evidence assistant. It uses local listing data and comparable-property analysis to produce a first-pass price range, supporting comps, and confidence caveats. I am looking for 5 Malta professionals to test whether it saves research time in real valuation work. It is not meant to replace formal valuations. Would you be open to testing it on 2-3 recent properties?

Week 3: Run concierge beta.

- Ask each participant for 2-3 properties they recently valued, reviewed, or found difficult.
- Generate PriceMap output, manually review it, and send the result within 24 hours.
- Ask for a forced rating: usable, directionally useful, not useful, or wrong.
- Ask why: bad comps, missing data, condition mismatch, locality mismatch, range too wide, methodology concern, or liability concern.

Week 4: Test willingness to pay.

- Offer one of two beta pilots:
  - EUR 49/month for 20 reviewed properties.
  - EUR 9 per reviewed property report.
- Treat payment willingness as validation, not revenue optimization.

## Product Requirements For Beta

Minimum report content:

- Property summary.
- Estimate range and price per square metre.
- 5-10 comparable properties with source, distance/locality, area, price, and listing date where available.
- Confidence label and reasons for lower confidence.
- Listing history or price-change context where available.
- Data provenance and limitations.
- Clear professional-use disclaimer.

Feedback fields:

- Output rating.
- Would this save research time?
- Would you cite any of these comps?
- Which comparable is wrong and why?
- What data would make this defensible?
- Would you pay for repeated access?

## Decision Rules

- If 3 or more professionals use it repeatedly, continue building professional workflow features: report export, comp filtering, provenance, and audit trail.
- If professionals like comparable discovery but distrust the estimate, reposition around comparable search and evidence packs.
- If professionals reject listing-based analysis because transaction data is mandatory, prioritize PPR/closed-sale data access before broader marketing.
- If professional uptake is weak but buyer demand is strong, keep B2C as a secondary validation path.

## Current Product Constraints

- Malta data currently comes primarily from RE/MAX Malta and MaltaPark listing data.
- PPR transaction data is identified as valuable but restricted/paid, not a current integrated data source.
- Current Malta model quality is useful for first-pass support, but should not be marketed as formal valuation quality.
